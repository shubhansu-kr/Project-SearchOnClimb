Kedarkantha Trek

₹10,000 ₹7999
The Kedarkantha Trek, often hailed as the Queen of Winter Treks, offers an unforgettable blend of adventure, breathtaking Himalayan landscapes, and cultural experiences, making it perfect for both beginners and seasoned trekkers. The journey begins in Sankri, a picturesque village that provides a glimpse into the rich local culture before leading to Juda Ka Talab, a stunning high-altitude lake that turns into a mesmerizing frozen spectacle in winter, adding a mystical charm with its mythological significance. As you ascend, the Kedarkantha summit rewards you with a breathtaking 360-degree panoramic view of the Himalayan range, where you can spot iconic peaks like Swargarohini, Bandarpunch, Black Peak, and Draupadi ka Danda 2, along with stunning vistas of Har Ki Doon Valley, Rupin Valley, Gangotri, and Yamunotri mountain ranges. Unlike more strenuous treks, Kedarkantha’s well-defined trails offer a moderate challenge, making it an ideal choice for beginners while still delivering awe-inspiring views at every turn. One of the most refreshing aspects of this trek is its no-network zone beyond Sankri, allowing trekkers to disconnect from technology and fully immerse themselves in nature’s tranquility.

Highlights

  • Sankri – Base Camp for Kedarkantha Trek: Sankri, a picturesque village in Uttarkashi, Uttarakhand, is the starting point of the Kedarkantha Trek. Known for its traditional wooden houses and breathtaking Himalayan views, Sankri also serves as a gateway to treks like Har Ki Dun, Phullara Ridge, and Bali Pass. This offbeat trekking destination is perfect for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.
  • Juda Ka Talab – A Mesmerizing High-Altitude Lake: Juda Ka Talab is a scenic high-altitude lake along the Kedarkantha Trek route, surrounded by dense forests of Rhododendron, Maple, Oak, and Pine trees. In winter, the frozen lake, blanketed by snow, offers a magical view. According to local folklore, Lord Shiva created this sacred lake, making it a must-visit spot for trekkers and nature photographers.
  • Kedarkantha Base – A Perfect Stargazing Spot: The Kedarkantha Base is a favorite campsite for trekkers due to its serene atmosphere and wide-open spaces. Nestled amidst the Himalayas, this site is ideal for stargazing, offering clear night skies without any artificial light pollution. This makes it a top camping destination for astrophotography and experiencing nature in its purest form.
  • Kedarkantha Peak – The Ultimate Himalayan Summit: Standing at 12,500 ft, Kedarkantha Peak is the highlight of the trek. The summit rewards trekkers with breathtaking panoramic views of snow-covered Himalayan ranges. The peak is home to small temples dedicated to Lord Shiva, Goddess Parvati, and Lord Ganesha. Witnessing the sunrise here is an unforgettable experience, as the sky turns into a mesmerizing blend of orange and red hues.
  • Hargaon Base – A Tranquil Campsite Amidst the Mountains: Hargaon Base is a peaceful campsite surrounded by alpine trees and offering distant views of majestic Himalayan peaks. In winter, this campsite is covered in snow, making it a dreamlike setting. The morning view from Hargaon is truly stunning, with mist-covered valleys and a refreshing mountain breeze, making it one of the best places to camp during the trek.
