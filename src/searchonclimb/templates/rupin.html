Rupin Pass Trek

₹20,000 ₹16499
The Rupin Pass Trek is a thrilling high-altitude trek that takes you through some of the most diverse and breathtaking landscapes in the Himalayas. Starting from Bawta in Himachal Pradesh and ending in Sangla, this trek is filled with scenic surprises—from towering Himalayan peaks, cascading waterfalls, lush forests, glacial meadows, and charming mountain hamlets, to the iconic hanging village of Jhaka. What makes Rupin Pass truly special is the ever-changing terrain. Trekkers get to experience all kinds of landscapes—from green valleys and warm foothills to icy slopes, rocky trails, and vast snowfields as they ascend to the pass. Along the way, the journey includes walking beside gushing rivers, crossing wooden bridges, and traversing rugged cliffs that add an adventurous edge to the trek. Despite its steep climbs and challenging trails, the breathtaking 360-degree views make every step worth the effort. Rupin Pass is perfect for seasoned trekkers seeking a thrilling Himalayan expedition with a mix of adventure, natural beauty, and cultural encounters in remote villages.

Highlights

  • Magnificent Waterfalls: Witness countless waterfalls and streams throughout the trek. The Rupin Valley is especially famous for its cascading waterfalls that plunge from great heights, creating the illusion of water falling straight from the sky.
  • Jakha – The Hanging Village: Perched dramatically at the edge of a mountain, Jakha Village appears as though it is suspended from a cliff, making it one of the most unique settlements along the trail.
  • Thrilling Snow Bridges: Experience the exhilaration of walking over naturally formed snow bridges. These stunning formations appear multiple times along the trek, adding to the adventure.
  • Enchanting Forest Trails: Trek through dense forests of fir, rhododendron, and oak. The sudden transitions between open landscapes and thick woodlands create an almost cinematic trekking experience.
  • Kinnaur Temple – A Cultural Gem: Visit the remarkable two-story temple in Sewa, which reflects the rich architectural and cultural heritage of Kinnaur, standing distinctively apart from its surroundings.
  • Stunning Views of Kinner Kailash: As you approach the final leg of the trek, be awestruck by the majestic views of the revered Kinner Kailash range, a sight that will leave you spellbound.
  • An Unforgettable Adventure with TTH: Join the TTH team for an expertly guided journey through the Rupin Pass Trek. We ensure a well-organized, safe, and immersive experience, allowing you to enjoy the adventure to the fullest.

How to Reach

  • Nearest Bus Stand: Dehradun / Rishikesh.
  • Nearest Railway Station: Dehradun / Haridwar.
  • Nearest Airport: Jolly Grant Airport, Dehradun.
  • Starting Point: Typically begins at a nearby village accessible by road.

Itinerary

  • Day-1: Arrival In Bawta
    • Altitude: 2,350 m/ 7,700 ft
    • Drive Distance: 195 km
  • Day-2: Bawta to Jakha
    • Altitude: 2,750 m/ 9,000 ft
    • Trek Distance: 5 km
  • Day-3: Jakha to Dandreyash Thatch
    • Altitude: 3,550m/ 11,700ft
    • Trek Distance: 11 km
  • Day-4: Dandreyash Thatch to Upper Waterfall
    • Altitude: 4,050m/ 13,300 ft
    • Trek Distance: 2.5 km
  • Day-5: Upper Waterfall to Ronti Gad, cross Rupin Pass
    • Altitude:
      • Rupin Pass: 4,700 m/ 15,350ft
      • Ronti Gad: 3,750 m/ 12,300 ft
    • Trek Distance: 12 km
  • Day-6: Rest / Acclimatization / Buffer day
    • Used as a rest, acclimatization, or buffer day
    • Can be used at any camp, depending on trekker’s fitness and weather
    • Generally used at Dandreyash Thatch camp
  • Day-7: Ronti Gad to Sangla & Drive to Shimla
    • Altitude: 2,650 m/ 8,700 ft (Sangla)
    • Trek Distance: 11 km
    • Drive Distance: 215 km

Necessary Items

  • Trekking shoes, warm clothing, and waterproof jackets.
  • Basic first aid kit and medications for altitude sickness.
  • Trekking poles, headlamp, and reusable water bottles.
  • Snacks, energy bars, and a power bank.

Local Culture
