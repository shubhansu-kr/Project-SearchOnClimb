Hampta Pass Trek

₹15,000 ₹11999
The Hampta Pass Trek is a breathtaking crossover trek that transitions from the lush green valleys of Kullu-Manali to the stark, arid landscapes of Chandra Valley in Lahaul. This trek offers a rare blend of diverse terrains, rich cultures, and thrilling adventure, making it a must-experience for trekking enthusiasts. The trail takes you through rugged rocky paths, sprawling meadows, cascading waterfalls, glacial streams, and the mesmerizing crescent-shaped Chandratal Lake. One of the highlights of this trek is the stunning convergence of three significant mountain passes—Hampta Pass, Rohtang Pass, and the gateway to Spiti. This creates an extraordinary visual contrast and a surreal experience as you traverse through changing landscapes. Additionally, trekkers get a glimpse into the unique Buddhist culture and traditions of remote Himalayan villages like Sethan, where the inhabitants are originally migrants from Tibet and Spiti Valley. Hampta Pass is an excellent trek for nature lovers and adventure seekers, as the terrain transforms dramatically at every turn. The thrilling climb to the pass adds to the excitement, and once at the summit, you can witness the striking difference between the lush Kullu Valley behind you and the barren, rugged expanse of Lahaul ahead. With its mix of stunning scenery, cultural immersion, and moderate difficulty level, Hampta Pass stands out as one of the best Himalayan treks for both beginners and seasoned trekkers.

Highlights

  • Scenic Himalayan Villages: Experience the charm of remote mountain villages on this trek. Sethan Village, located at 8,860 ft, is a serene Buddhist hamlet near Manali, surrounded by the stunning Dhauladhar Ranges. Just below it, Hampta Village holds mythological significance, believed to be the route Yudhishthir took on his journey to heaven.
  • Exciting Hampta Pass Crossing: One of the most thrilling sections of the trek is crossing the Hampta Pass (4,300 m). Trekkers ascend three steep ledges before reaching the vast mountain pass, which dramatically drops into the breathtaking Lahaul Valley.
  • Diverse Terrain Transitions: This crossover trek showcases a striking contrast between two landscapes—the lush greenery of the Kullu Valley and the arid, rugged terrain of Chandra Valley in Lahaul. The journey unfolds like a natural spectacle, making it a paradise for adventure seekers.
  • Stunning Campsites: The trek features some of the most picturesque camping locations. Balu Ka Ghera sits beside a river with a breathtaking view of Hampta Pass, while Shea Goru offers a lush green setting with a meandering stream and surrounding snow-clad peaks.
  • Rich Himalayan Flora and Fauna: The trail passes through dense forests of Deodar, Pine, Maple, and Oak, while birdwatchers can spot exotic Himalayan species like the White-Capped Redstart and Whistling Thrush. Shepherd dogs and herds of sheep further add to the wilderness experience.
  • Breathtaking Mountain Views: Witness panoramic vistas of the Dhauladhar and Pir Panjal Ranges. From Hampta Pass, trekkers can admire the majestic peaks of Mt. Indrasen, Deo Tibba, and surrounding glaciers.
  • Chandratal Lake – The Moon Lake: A highlight of the trek is the visit to the mesmerizing Chandratal Lake, a stunning high-altitude water body encircled by barren mountains. Known for its crescent shape and ever-changing colors, the lake is steeped in Hindu mythology, believed to be the spot where Yudhishthir ascended to heaven.

How to Reach

  • Nearest Bus Stand: Dehradun / Rishikesh.
  • Nearest Railway Station: Dehradun / Haridwar.
  • Nearest Airport: Jolly Grant Airport, Dehradun.
  • Starting Point: Typically begins at a nearby village accessible by road.

Itinerary

  • Day 1: Manali to Chika via Jobra | Walk Through Dense Forests
    Begin your journey from Manali and drive to Jobra, a scenic spot at 3,000m. The trek to Chika offers a refreshing walk through dense pine and maple forests, opening up to lush meadows surrounded by towering peaks.
    Altitude: Manali - 2,100m | Jobra - 3,000m | Chika - 3,100m
    Drive Distance: 16 km (Manali to Jobra)
    Trek Distance: 3 km (Jobra to Chika)
  • Day 2: Chika to Balu Ka Ghera | Camp Amidst Majestic Landscapes
    Trek through the mesmerizing valley, crossing glacial streams and boulder-laden trails. Balu Ka Ghera, a vast open ground at 3,800m, offers stunning views of the surrounding peaks.
    Trek Distance: 9 km
  • Day 3: Balu Ka Ghera to Shea Goru via Hampta Pass | Panoramic Mountain Views
    Experience the thrill of crossing Hampta Pass at 4,300m, offering panoramic views of the Himalayas. Descend to the picturesque valley of Shea Goru, a serene campsite surrounded by rugged terrain.
    Trek Distance: 7 km
  • Day 4: Shea Goru to Chatru and Drive to Chandratal | Bask in the Serenity of the Lake
    A scenic trek to Chatru leads to an adventurous drive to the mesmerizing Chandratal Lake, famous for its ever-changing hues and breathtaking reflections of the surrounding peaks.
    Trek Distance: 7 km
    Drive Distance: 50 km (to Chandratal and back)
  • Day 5: Chatru to Manali | Farewell to the Himalayas
    Drive back to Manali, reminiscing about the unforgettable journey through the Hampta Pass and beyond.
    Drive Distance: 65 km

Necessary Items

  • Trekking shoes, warm clothing, and waterproof jackets.
  • Basic first aid kit and medications for altitude sickness.
  • Trekking poles, headlamp, and reusable water bottles.
  • Snacks, energy bars, and a power bank.

Local Culture

    During the trek, you'll pass through remote Himalayan villages where you can experience the rich culture and traditions of Uttarakhand. Interact with the locals to learn about their unique way of life, cuisine, and folklore.
