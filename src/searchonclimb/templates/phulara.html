Phulara Ridge Trek

₹13,000 ₹8499
Stretching approximately 30 km, the Phulara Ridge Trek in Uttarakhand offers a spectacular blend of alpine meadows, dense forests, and breathtaking Himalayan views. Unlike conventional treks that weave through valleys and forests, this trail takes you along an exposed ridgeline, where the vastness of nature surrounds you. The trek is especially captivating in spring and summer, when the trail is covered in blooming alpine flowers, while autumn paints the landscape in golden hues. Moreover, Govind Pashu Vihar’s rich biodiversity makes this trek perfect for wildlife lovers and birdwatchers, offering a chance to spot rare Himalayan species. With its perfect mix of adventure, solitude, and awe-inspiring landscapes, the Phulara Ridge Trek is a must-visit for those seeking an offbeat trekking experience in India.

Highlights

  • Summit Views – Panoramic Himalayan Vistas: The Phulara Ridge Trek offers breathtaking panoramic views of the Himalayan peaks from the summit and along the trail. Trekkers can witness stunning vistas of Swargarohini, Kala Nag, Devkyara, Bandarpoonch, Rangnala, Hanuman Top, Top of Sarutal, Vijay Top, and Kedarkantha. This ridge trek is a paradise for mountain lovers and photographers.
  • Sankri – The Base Camp for Phulara Ridge Trek: Sankri, the starting point of the Phulara Ridge Trek, serves as a base camp for over 25 treks in the Uttarakhand Himalayas. Located 200 km from Dehradun, Sankri is surrounded by lush green valleys, towering pine forests, and rivers like the Yamuna, Tons, and Kedarganga. It has a rich history of trekking, with foreign and Indian trekkers frequently visiting since the 1970s. Today, Sankri is one of the most visited trekking base camps in India.
  • Pushtara Meadows – A Hidden Gem of the Himalayas: After descending from the mountain, trekkers arrive at the breathtaking Pushtara Meadows. These vast green fields stretch endlessly, covered in a lush carpet of grass and blooming wildflowers. The meadows offer a spectacular 270-degree view of snow-capped Himalayan peaks, making it one of the most scenic spots on the trek.
  • The Forests – A Walk Through Dense Himalayan Woods: Despite being a short trek, Phulara Ridge boasts incredibly dense forests. The thick canopy allows only streaks of sunlight to filter through, creating a mystical ambiance. The trail is often untouched, offering a serene and immersive experience. The forest is home to towering oaks, pines, dwarf rhododendrons, silver birches, and maple trees, making it a haven for nature lovers.
  • Bird Watching – Spot the Rare Himalayan Monal: The dense forests of Phulara Ridge provide an ideal habitat for diverse bird species. Trekkers may get a rare glimpse of the vibrant Himalayan Monal, the state bird of Uttarakhand. Other species like barbets, woodpeckers, and Himalayan whistling thrushes can also be spotted, making this trek a must-visit for birdwatching enthusiasts.

How to Reach

  • Nearest Bus Stand: Dehradun / Rishikesh.
  • Nearest Railway Station: Dehradun / Haridwar.
  • Nearest Airport: Jolly Grant Airport, Dehradun.
  • Starting Point: Typically begins at a nearby village accessible by road.

Itinerary

  • Dehradun to Sankri
    Altitude (Sankri): 1,950m / 6,400 ft.
    Drive Distance: 220 km.
  • Sankri to Sikolta
    Altitude (Sikolta): 2,850m / 9,400 ft.
    Trek Distance: 7 km.
  • Sikolta to Bhoj Gadi
    Altitude: 11,115 ft.
    Trek Distance: 4.45 km.
  • Bhoj Gadi to Pushtara via Phulara Ridge
    Altitude: Pushtara - 9,500 ft, Phulara Ridge - 12,150 ft.
    Trek Distance: 8.4 km.
  • Pushtara to Taluka to Sankri
    Altitude: Taluka - 6,919 ft, Sankri - 6,400 ft.
    Trek Distance (Pushtara to Taluka): 8.1 km.
  • Sankri to Dehradun
    Drive Distance: 220 km.

Necessary Items

  • Trekking shoes, warm clothing, and waterproof jackets.
  • Basic first aid kit and medications for altitude sickness.
  • Trekking poles, headlamp, and reusable water bottles.
  • Snacks, energy bars, and a power bank.

Local Culture

    During the trek, you'll pass through remote Himalayan villages where you can experience the rich culture and traditions of Uttarakhand. Interact with the locals to learn about their unique way of life, cuisine, and folklore.
