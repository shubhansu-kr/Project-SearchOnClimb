Bali Pass Trek

₹25,000 ₹20999
The Bali Pass Trek is a hidden gem in the Indian Himalayas, offering a perfect blend of adventure, stunning landscapes, and spiritual significance. Despite being an offbeat trek, it takes you through well-known locations such as Ruinsara Tal, Har Ki Dun Valley, Osla Village, and the revered Yamunotri Dham, one of the four sacred Dhams. A unique aspect of this trek is that it serves as a connecting route between Har Ki Dun Valley and Yamunotri, providing breathtaking views of Bandarpoonch, Swargarohini, and other towering peaks of the Garhwal Himalayas. This high-altitude Himalayan trek is packed with thrilling challenges, making it an excellent choice for adventure seekers. The trail offers unobstructed panoramic views of snow-capped peaks, alpine meadows, dense forests, and serene glacial lakes. Starting from Sankri and concluding at Jankichatti, the trek spans eight days, featuring steep ascents, technical descents, and narrow trails, ensuring an adrenaline-filled journey. Due to its challenging nature, the Bali Pass Trek is best suited for experienced trekkers looking to test their endurance. Proper training and preparation for at least three to four months are highly recommended before embarking on this trek. The combination of diverse terrains, extreme altitudes, and raw natural beauty makes this a must-visit for those seeking an offbeat Himalayan trekking experience. Whether it's the thrill of conquering 16,200 ft, the breathtaking mountain vistas, or the chance to explore untouched wilderness, Bali Pass offers an unforgettable Himalayan adventure.

Highlights

  • Ruinsara Tal - The Enchanting Glacial Lake
    One of the most mesmerizing highlights of the Bali Pass Trek, Ruinsara Tal is a breathtaking glacial lake nestled among the majestic Swargrohini peaks. Its serene ambiance, complemented by lush meadows and vibrant Rhododendron shrubs, creates a picture-perfect retreat that radiates tranquility.
  • Osla and Gangad - The Ancient Himalayan Villages
    Step back in time as you journey through the historic villages of Osla and Gangad, believed to date back to the Mahabharata era. Experience the rich cultural heritage, marvel at the unique Himalayan architectural style, and visit the iconic Osla temple. Engage with the locals and immerse yourself in their fascinating folklore.
  • Devsu Thatch - The Pristine Alpine Meadows
    A paradise for nature lovers, the alpine meadows of Devsu Thatch offer a stunning landscape surrounded by towering peaks like Swargrohini and Kalanag. The lush greenery, dotted with colorful alpine flowers, makes it a perfect spot to soak in the beauty of the Himalayas.

How to Reach

  • Nearest Bus Stand: Dehradun / Rishikesh.
  • Nearest Railway Station: Dehradun / Haridwar.
  • Nearest Airport: Jolly Grant Airport, Dehradun.
  • Starting Point: Typically begins at a nearby village accessible by road.

Itinerary

  • Day 1: Drive from Dehradun to Sankri
    Altitude: 1,950 m (6,400 ft)
    Distance: 220 km
  • Day 2: Sankri to Seema via Taluka
    Altitude: Taluka - 2,000 m (6,700 ft), Seema - 2,600 m (8,500 ft)
    Drive Distance: 12 km (Sankri to Taluka)
    Trek Distance: 12 km (Taluka to Seema)
  • Day 3: Seema to Devsu Bugyal
    Altitude: 3,100 m (10,200 ft)
    Trek Distance: 10 km
  • Day 4: Devsu Bugyal to Ruinsara Tal
    Altitude: 3,600 m (11,800 ft)
    Trek Distance: 7 km
  • Day 5: Ruinsara Tal to Odari
    Altitude: 4,000 m (13,100 ft)
    Trek Distance: 4 km
  • Day 6: Odari to Bali Col Camp
    Altitude: 4,600 m (15,100 ft)
    Trek Distance: 5 km
  • Day 7: Bali Col Camp to Lower Dhamni via Bali Pass
    Altitude: Bali Pass - 4,950 m (16,200 ft), Lower Dhamni - 3,350 m (11,100 ft)
    Trek Distance: 14 km
  • Day 8: Lower Dhamni to Janki Chatti, Drive to Dehradun
    Altitude: Janki Chatti - 2,600 m (8,500 ft)
    Trek Distance: 8 km (Lower Dhamni to Janki Chatti)
    Drive Distance: 170 km (Janki Chatti to Dehradun)

Necessary Items

  • Trekking shoes, warm clothing, and waterproof jackets.
  • Basic first aid kit and medications for altitude sickness.
  • Trekking poles, headlamp, and reusable water bottles.
  • Snacks, energy bars, and a power bank.
