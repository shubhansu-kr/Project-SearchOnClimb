Sar Pass Trek

₹13,000 ₹10499
The Sar Pass Trek, standing at an altitude of 13,799 ft, is one of the most scenic and thrilling treks in Himachal Pradesh. This 50 km trail, graded as easy to moderate, begins in the picturesque village of Kasol and concludes at Barshaini, taking trekkers through lush pine forests, expansive meadows, and breathtaking snow-capped mountains. Starting from the serene Parvati Valley, the trek begins with a hike to Grahan Village, known for its traditional Himachali charm. From Grahan, the trail ascends to Min Thach, a stunning meadow, followed by a trek to Nagaru, the highest campsite on the route. The next leg of the journey takes you to Biskeri Thach, another mesmerizing grassland, offering panoramic views of the surrounding peaks. The highlight of the trek is crossing Sar Pass, where the landscape transforms into a pristine, snow-covered wonderland, providing an exhilarating experience. Sar Pass Trek is an excellent choice for adventure seekers and nature lovers, offering a perfect blend of diverse landscapes, moderate challenges, and breathtaking views, making it one of the best treks in Himachal Pradesh.

Highlights

  • Altitude & Difficulty: Sar Pass is located at an altitude of 13,799 ft and is an easy to moderate-grade trek, making it perfect for beginners and adventure seekers.
  • Trek Distance & Route: Covering approximately 50 km, the trek begins in Kasol and ends at Barshaini, passing through lush pine forests, alpine meadows, and snow-clad peaks.
  • Scenic Trail: The journey starts in Kasol, a quaint village in the Parvati Valley, leading to the picturesque Grahan Village. From there, trekkers ascend to Min Thach, a stunning meadow, followed by a trek to Nagaru and Biskeri Thach.
  • Enchanting Forests: The trail is surrounded by dense forests of Pine, Deodar, and Rhododendrons, offering a refreshing and serene trekking experience.
  • Starry Night Experience: A highlight of the trek is camping under a mesmerizing night sky filled with millions of stars—an unforgettable experience for nature lovers and photographers.
  • Varied Landscapes: One of the most fascinating aspects of Sar Pass Trek is its changing scenery. It begins with lush green vegetation and gradually transitions into a breathtaking snow-covered paradise.
  • Diverse Terrains: The trek takes you across a mix of terrains, including grassy meadows, dense woodlands, and snowy stretches, making it an exciting and diverse adventure.
  • Perfect for Beginners: The manageable difficulty level makes Sar Pass an ideal choice for first-time trekkers who want to experience the magic of Himalayan trekking without extreme challenges.
  • Must-Do Himalayan Adventure: With its stunning landscapes, thrilling high-altitude crossings, and captivating village trails, Sar Pass is a trek that should be on every adventure enthusiast’s bucket list.

How to Reach

  • Nearest Bus Stand: Dehradun / Rishikesh.
  • Nearest Railway Station: Dehradun / Haridwar.
  • Nearest Airport: Jolly Grant Airport, Dehradun.
  • Starting Point: Typically begins at a nearby village accessible by road.

Itinerary

  • Day 1: Kasol to Grahan Village
    • Altitude: Kasol - 1,700 m | Grahan - 2,350 m
    • Altitude Gain: 650 m
    • Drive Distance: 8 km | Duration: 45 minutes
    • Trek Distance: 2 km | Duration: 1 hour
  • Day 2: Grahan to Min Thach
    • Altitude: 3,400 m
    • Altitude Gain: 1,050 m
    • Trek Distance: 10 km | Duration: 5-6 hours
    • Terrain: First 9 km is a steep ascent, followed by a 1 km descent
    • Highlights: Walk through dense forests of Rhododendrons, Pine, and Golden Oak
    • Water Source: Available at Min Thach campsite (groundwater tap)
  • Day 3: Min Thach to Nagaru
    • Altitude: 3,800 m
    • Altitude Gain: 400 m
    • Trek Distance: 6 km | Duration: 4-6 hours
    • Terrain: Continuous ascent
  • Day 4: Nagaru to Biskeri Thach via Sar Pass
    • Altitude: Sar Pass - 4,200 m | Biskeri Thach - 3,350 m
    • Altitude Gain: 400 m
    • Trek Distance: 14 km | Duration: 6-8 hours
    • Terrain: 5 km steep ascent on snow till Sar Pass top
  • Day 5: Biskeri Thach to Barshaini and Drive Back to Kasol
    • Altitude: 2,400 m
    • Altitude Loss: 950 m
    • Trek Distance: 10 km | Duration: 4-5 hours
    • Drive Distance: 15 km | Duration: 40 min - 1 hour
    • Terrain: Entire trek is a descent
    • Pick-up Point: Last 2 km on an off-road stretch till the power project
    • Additional Sightseeing: Visit Gurudwara Sahib Manikaran & hot springs (extra taxi waiting charges apply)
    • Final Drop: Reach Kasol by 4-5 PM | Drive along the scenic Parvati River

Necessary Items

  • Trekking shoes, warm clothing, and waterproof jackets.
  • Basic first aid kit and medications for altitude sickness.
  • Trekking poles, headlamp, and reusable water bottles.
  • Snacks, energy bars, and a power bank.
